Animals Laws

In India, pet ownership is primarily governed by the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals (PCA) Act, 1960, alongside guidelines issued by the Animal Welfare Board of India (AWBI) and various municipal bylaws. The core principle is humane treatment, mandating that pet owners provide adequate food, water, shelter, and veterinary care, and refrain from any act of cruelty, neglect, or abandonment. Violations can lead to fines and/or imprisonment under the PCA Act and sections of the Indian Penal Code (IPC), specifically Sections 428 and 429 for harming animals, and Section 503 for threatening animal caregivers.
