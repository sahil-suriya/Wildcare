The Wildlife Protection Act, 1972

The Wildlife Protection Act, 1972 is a key legislation in India aimed at protecting wild animals, birds, and plants. It provides legal framework for the conservation of wildlife and the establishment of protected areas like National Parks and Wildlife Sanctuaries.

Forest Conservation Act, 1980

The Forest Conservation Act, 1980 was enacted by the Government of India to prevent deforestation and ensure the sustainable use of forest resources. The Act aims to conserve forests by restricting the use of forest land for non-forest purposes (like industries, roads, or agriculture).

Environment Protection Act, 1986

The Environment Protection Act, 1986 was enacted after the Bhopal Gas Tragedy to provide a strong framework for environmental protection in India. It gives wide powers to the central government to take necessary actions to protect the environment and public health.
